# Module 8: Noise in Measurement Systems

## Overview

Noise is an ever-present challenge in measurement systems, limiting the achievable accuracy and resolution. Understanding the sources, characteristics, and mitigation of noise is essential for designing and operating precision measurement systems. This module covers the fundamental types of noise encountered in electronic measurements and the techniques used to minimize their impact.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

1. Identify and characterize different types of noise in measurement systems
2. Understand the physical origins of thermal, shot, and flicker noise
3. Calculate signal-to-noise ratio for measurement systems
4. Apply appropriate noise reduction techniques for different situations

## Topics Covered

### Lesson 1: Types of Noise
- Thermal (Johnson-Nyquist) noise
- Shot noise
- Flicker (1/f) noise
- Noise power spectral density

### Lesson 2: Noise Reduction Techniques
- Signal-to-noise ratio fundamentals
- Shielding and grounding practices
- Analog and digital filtering
- Averaging and signal processing methods

## Prerequisites

- Understanding of basic circuit theory
- Knowledge of AC signals and frequency response
- Familiarity with measurement fundamentals (Modules 1-3)
