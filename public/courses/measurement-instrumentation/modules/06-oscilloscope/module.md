# Module 6: The Oscilloscope

## Overview

The oscilloscope is one of the most versatile and essential instruments in electrical and electronic measurement. This module covers the fundamental principles of oscilloscope operation, from basic analog concepts to modern digital storage oscilloscopes (DSOs). Understanding oscilloscope operation is crucial for anyone working with time-varying signals, circuit debugging, and waveform analysis.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

1. Explain the basic operation and block diagram of an oscilloscope
2. Understand the vertical and horizontal deflection systems
3. Describe the principles of digital storage oscilloscope operation
4. Apply bandwidth and rise time relationships in oscilloscope selection

## Topics Covered

### Lesson 1: Oscilloscope Fundamentals
- Basic oscilloscope operation principles
- Block diagram analysis
- Vertical deflection system
- Horizontal deflection system and time base
- Triggering concepts

### Lesson 2: Digital Storage Oscilloscopes
- DSO architecture and operation
- Sampling theory and techniques
- Bandwidth considerations
- Rise time and bandwidth relationship
- Memory depth and waveform capture

## Prerequisites

- Understanding of basic electrical circuits
- Knowledge of AC and DC signals
- Familiarity with measurement fundamentals (Modules 1-5)
