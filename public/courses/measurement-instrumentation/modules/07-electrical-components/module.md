# Module 7: Measurement of Electrical Components

## Overview

Accurate measurement of electrical component parameters is fundamental to circuit design, quality control, and troubleshooting. This module covers the principles and techniques for measuring resistance and impedance, including both traditional bridge methods and modern digital instruments. Understanding these measurement methods enables selection of appropriate techniques for different applications and accuracy requirements.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

1. Apply two-wire and four-wire resistance measurement techniques
2. Analyze Wheatstone bridge circuits for precision resistance measurement
3. Understand impedance measurement using LCR meters
4. Interpret Q factor and dissipation factor measurements

## Topics Covered

### Lesson 1: Resistance Measurement
- Two-wire measurement technique and limitations
- Four-wire (Kelvin) measurement principle
- Wheatstone bridge configuration
- Bridge sensitivity and accuracy considerations

### Lesson 2: Impedance Measurement
- Complex impedance fundamentals
- LCR meter operating principles
- Q factor measurement and significance
- Dissipation factor (tan Î´) interpretation

## Prerequisites

- Understanding of DC circuit theory
- Knowledge of AC fundamentals
- Familiarity with measurement errors (Module 2)
