# Fourier Series

## Module Overview

The Fourier Series is a powerful mathematical tool that allows any periodic signal to be expressed as a sum of sinusoidal components. This module provides the theoretical foundation for frequency-domain analysis and establishes the connection between time-domain waveforms and their spectral composition.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

1. Understand the concept of representing periodic signals as sums of sinusoids
2. Calculate Fourier series coefficients (a₀, aₙ, bₙ) for various periodic signals
3. Express Fourier series in complex exponential form
4. Analyze the spectral content of periodic signals using Fourier series

## Lessons

- **Lesson 1:** Fourier Series Introduction - Concept and trigonometric form
- **Lesson 2:** Fourier Coefficients - Methods for calculating a₀, aₙ, bₙ
- **Lesson 3:** Complex Exponential Form - Complex Fourier series representation

## Key Concepts

- **Fourier Series:** Representation of periodic signals as sum of harmonically related sinusoids
- **Spectral Components:** Discrete frequency components at multiples of fundamental frequency
- **Fourier Coefficients:** Weights determining the contribution of each harmonic
- **Complex Form:** Compact representation using complex exponentials

## Prerequisites

- Understanding of periodic signals
- Knowledge of sinusoidal signals and complex exponentials
- Integration techniques
